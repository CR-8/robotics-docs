# Chassis Design

The chassis is the foundation of any combat robot, providing structure, protection, and mounting for components.

## Design Considerations

### Weight Classes

**Beetleweight**: 3 lbs (1.36 kg)
- Compact design
- 3D printed parts common
- Brushless motors

**Antweight**: 1 lb (454 g)
- Ultra-compact
- Minimal armor
- High power density

**Featherweight**: 30 lbs (13.6 kg)
- Increased complexity
- Better armor options
- More powerful weapons

**Hobbyweight**: 12 lbs (5.4 kg)
**Lightweight**: 60 lbs (27.2 kg)
**Middleweight**: 120 lbs (54.4 kg)
**Heavyweight**: 250 lbs (113.4 kg)

### Material Selection

**Aluminum (6061-T6)**
- ✅ Lightweight
- ✅ Easy to machine
- ✅ Good strength-to-weight
- ❌ Moderate impact resistance
- **Use**: Frame, internal structure

**HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)**
- ✅ Excellent impact absorption
- ✅ Self-lubricating
- ✅ Cheap
- ❌ Melts under heat
- **Use**: Wedges, armor, wheels

**AR500/AR400 Steel**
- ✅ Extreme hardness
- ✅ Impact resistant
- ❌ Very heavy
- **Use**: Spinner teeth, critical armor

**Titanium**
- ✅ Best strength-to-weight
- ✅ Spark resistance
- ❌ Very expensive
- ❌ Hard to machine
- **Use**: High-end spinner bars

**TPU (3D Printed)**
- ✅ Flexible, absorbs impacts
- ✅ Fast iteration
- ❌ Limited strength
- **Use**: Shock mounts, protective covers

**Carbon Fiber**
- ✅ Extremely light
- ✅ Very strong (in tension)
- ❌ Brittle (poor impact)
- ❌ Expensive
- **Use**: Spinner discs, structural panels

## Chassis Types

### Wedge/Ramp Bot

```
     /|
    / |
   /  |
  /   |
 /____|
[MOTOR][BATTERY]
```

**Pros**:
- Simple design
- Effective against many bots
- Low center of gravity

**Cons**:
- Passive weapon
- Vulnerable to spinners

### Vertical Spinner

```
    [SPINNER]
    /       \
   |  [BAT]  |
   |_[MOTOR]_|
    W       W
```

**Pros**:
- High kinetic energy
- Offensive capability
- Intimidating

**Cons**:
- Complex weapon system
- Gyroscopic effects
- High current draw

### Horizontal Spinner

```
================
   [SPINNER]
 ___________
|[M][B][M] |
|_W_____W__|
```

**Pros**:
- Maximum energy transfer
- 360° coverage
- Devastating hits

**Cons**:
- Reaction forces
- Exposed wheels
- Hard to control

### Drum Spinner

```
   ___TEETH___
  /           \
 |    [M]      |
 |   [BAT]    |
 |_____W_____W|
```

**Pros**:
- Good traction
- Continuous engagement
- Stable

**Cons**:
- Heavy weapon
- Complex construction

### Lifter/Flipper

```
    /====\  <- Arm
   |      |
   |[GAS] |  or  [MOTOR]
   |[BAT] |
   |__W_W_|
```

**Pros**:
- Control opponent
- OOTA (Out Of The Arena)
- Strategic

**Cons**:
- Requires precision
- Gas systems complex
- Limited damage

```python
# Calculate spinner kinetic energy
def spinner_energy(mass_kg, radius_m, rpm):
    omega = rpm * 2 * 3.14159 / 60  # rad/s
    moment_of_inertia = 0.5 * mass_kg * radius_m**2  # Disc approximation
    energy_joules = 0.5 * moment_of_inertia * omega**2
    return energy_joules

# Example: 3kg disc, 15cm radius, 10000 RPM
```

### 3. Center of Gravity

Keep COG low and centered:

```python
def calculate_cog(components):
    total_mass = sum(c['mass'] for c in components)
    cog_x = sum(c['mass'] * c['x'] for c in components) / total_mass
    cog_y = sum(c['mass'] * c['y'] for c in components) / total_mass
    cog_z = sum(c['mass'] * c['z'] for c in components) / total_mass
    return (cog_x, cog_y, cog_z)
```

## Armor Strategies

### Ablative Armor
- HDPE, UHMW
- Absorbs impacts by deforming
- Cheap to replace

### Hardened Armor
- AR500 steel, titanium
- Deflects hits
- Protects critical areas

### Sloped Armor
- Deflects spinners
- Reduces effective thickness needed
- Aerodynamic

## Drivetrain Layout

### 2WD (Two-Wheel Drive)
- ✅ Simple
- ✅ Lightweight
- ❌ Less traction
- ❌ Less pushing power

### 4WD (Four-Wheel Drive)
- ✅ Maximum traction
- ✅ Pushing power
- ❌ Heavier
- ❌ More complex

### Tank/Skid Steer
```cpp
void drive(int forward, int turn) {
  int left = forward + turn;
  int right = forward - turn;
  
  setLeftMotors(left);
  setRightMotors(right);
}
```

## Fasteners & Assembly

**Thread-locking**:
- Blue Loctite: Removable
- Red Loctite: Permanent

**Fastener Types**:
- Socket head cap screws: Strong, low profile
- Button head: Clearance for armor
- Countersunk: Flush mount

## Survival Tips

1. **Redundancy**: Dual motor controllers
2. **Protection**: Conformal coating on electronics
3. **Accessibility**: Easy battery access
4. **Shock Mounting**: Isolate electronics
5. **Cable Management**: Prevent wire snagging

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Overweight**: Always under-budget
❌ **High COG**: Will get flipped
❌ **Weak Fasteners**: Use grade 8 or better
❌ **No Weapon Safety**: Add removable link
❌ **Poor Wiring**: Loose wires = fires
❌ **Fragile Wheels**: Protect from spinners

## Building Process

1. CAD design + weight check
2. Order materials
3. CNC/waterjet cutting
4. Assemble chassis
5. Mount electronics
6. Test drive
7. Add weapon
8. Arena testing
9. Competition!
